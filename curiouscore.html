Overview

Background

CuriousCore is an education startup that specialises in helping mid-career professionals break into the User Experience (UX) Design industry, and helping business leaders to learn the fundamentals of Human-Centered Design (HCD). They offer a variety of UX programs, ranging from short, intensive courses to longer, part-time courses.

The Problem

In order to improve their courses, marketing and sales, CuriousCore wants to learn more about their prospective and current customers and improve the way information is displayed on their website.

Our Goal

The aim of this project was to understand the needs of CuriousCore customers, and to improve the user experience of the CuriousCore website.

Research

Competitive Analysis

Firstly, I conducted a competitor analysis of education companies - both in Singapore and around the world - that offer UX bootcamps and UX courses.

This competitor research provided us with a better understanding of the advantages and disadvantages of different UX programs and how they are marketed, which helped to guide CuriousCore's marketing and content strategy.

Surveys

To learn about the problems that visitors are facing on the CuriousCore website, I added a pop-up questionnaire on the CuriousCore website and sent out a Google Form to CuriousCore's mailing list.

The surveys showed that many of the website visitors were not able to obtain the information they needed. Some visitors also commented that the content was unclear and poorly organised.

User Interviews

To gain a deeper understanding of the issues encountered by visitors on the website, I conducted 9 remote user interviews (using Zoom) with 6 prospective customers and 3 current customers.

These interviews provided us with valuable insights into the attributes, challenges and goals of potential customers. Using these findings, I created three personas to capture the various profiles of potential customers.

I also conducted Affinity Mapping on the interview responses. After identifying the main themes underlying the usability issues, I worked together with the founder of CuriousCore to address these problems by editing the content and design of the website.

Design - Home Page

Problems:

  • The company description was confusing - website visitors did not know that CuriousCore taught courses in User Experience (UX).
  • There was information targeted at enterprises which was irrelevant and confusing to some visitors
  • Website visitors could not find the links to CuriousCore's social media pages.

Solutions:

  • The company description was edited to make it clear that CuriousCore was an educational platform that specialises user experience and digital design.
  • A separate page was created specifically "For Enterprises".
  • Links to CuriousCore's Facebook and LinkedIn pages were added to the footer.

Design - About Page

Problems:

  • Users were not able to understand what the company does from the About page.
  • There was also a lack of information to establish the reliability of the organisation.
  • The hero image was an irrelevant image of ocean waves.

Solutions:

  • We added a specific description of what CuriousCore does as a company ("we specialise in helping mid-career professionals break into the UX Design industry".)
  • To establish credibility, we added information about CuriousCore's founder, the CuriousCore team and their educators.
  • We replaced the irrelevant hero image with a photo of CuriousCore's founder with some happy customers.

Design - Webinars Page

The website had a page to advertise their weekly webinars.

Problems:

  • Important information such as the date and time of upcoming webinars couldn't be found easily.
  • Website visitors also did not know where or how to access the webinars.

Solutions:

  • An "overview" webinars page was created which shows the important information upfront. Visitors can navigate to a separate page to learn more.
  • A clear call-to-action was introduced to allow visitors to obtain the webinar link.

Design - Course Page

The website had a page to advertise their 2-Day UX Intensive Course.

Problems:

  • There was a confusing structure with too much information competing for attention.
  • There was too much text - many website visitors commented that they were "too lazy to read everything".
  • The testimonials from previous students were placed in many different places and lacked credibility.

Solutions:

  • I changed the structure a linear flow, rather than two columns of information competing for attention at once. The structure followed the order of what participants felt were the most important pieces of information.
  • The amount of text was reduced, and more images and whitespace was added.
  • Student testimonials were grouped together into a single section and the specific dates of when they took the course were added to increase their credibility.

Outcome & Reflections

Outcome

The start-up founder was pleased with the research deliverables I presented (competitive analysis, personas). My design recommendations were also implemented on the live website.

"Jingxiu has the gift of simplifying complex information and presenting data in a meaningful way to me as a business owner which was critical in helping me to understand our customers/prospects objectively."

Daylon, Founder of CuriousCore

Reflections

From this project, I learnt the value of using research to inform design. When I used evidence from user research to inform my design solutions, I was able to address the real problems faced by actual visitors of the website. Additionally, the stakeholder was more receptive to my design solutions when they were driven by data.

This project also taught me the importance of adapting to limitations in the real world. In particular, the design changes that could be made to the website were constrained by the hosting platform (SquareSpace). Hence, rather than obsessing over pixel-perfect designs, I focused more on feasible solutions - such as changes to page structure and content.
